# Lexical Chunks: Meditation & Mindfulness

*150 chunks | Level: Professional*

---

## Basic Practice

**1. sit in silence** — ngồi trong im lặng
*Dùng khi: mô tả hành động thiền cơ bản mỗi sáng*
> She starts every morning by sitting in silence for ten minutes before opening her laptop.
> The retreat required participants to sit in silence for three full days.
> Even five minutes of sitting in silence can reset your nervous system before a big meeting.

**2. close your eyes** — nhắm mắt lại
*Dùng khi: hướng dẫn bắt đầu một bài thiền ngắn*
> The facilitator asked everyone to close their eyes and take three slow breaths.
> Closing your eyes during a one-minute break helps signal to your brain that it's time to rest.
> He would close his eyes at his desk whenever the noise got overwhelming.

**3. anchor your attention** — neo chú ý vào một điểm
*Dùng khi: giải thích kỹ thuật tập trung tâm trí*
> In breath-based meditation, you anchor your attention on the sensation of air entering the nostrils.
> Anchoring your attention to the present task reduces the mental cost of context-switching.
> Skilled meditators can anchor their attention even in noisy, open-plan offices.

**4. body scan** — quét cơ thể / scan toàn thân
*Dùng khi: nói về kỹ thuật thư giãn từng phần cơ thể*
> A quick body scan before a board presentation helps you notice and release physical tension.
> She uses a five-minute body scan each evening to wind down from the workday.
> The app guides users through a body scan that moves from the feet up to the crown of the head.

**5. guided meditation** — thiền có hướng dẫn
*Dùng khi: giới thiệu hình thức thiền phổ biến cho người mới*
> He started with guided meditation on Calm before feeling confident enough to sit on his own.
> The company's wellness program includes a daily ten-minute guided meditation session.
> Guided meditation is a low-barrier entry point for executives who have never meditated before.

**6. breath awareness** — nhận thức về hơi thở
*Dùng khi: nói về nền tảng của hầu hết các kỹ thuật thiền*
> Breath awareness is the foundation of most mindfulness practices because the breath is always available.
> Even a sixty-second breath awareness exercise can interrupt a stress spiral.
> She teaches breath awareness as a core skill in her leadership coaching work.

**7. focused attention** — sự chú ý tập trung
*Dùng khi: phân biệt loại thiền tập trung vào một đối tượng cụ thể*
> Focused attention meditation trains the mind the same way lifting weights trains a muscle.
> Research shows that focused attention practice increases gray matter density in the prefrontal cortex.
> He uses focused attention techniques before writing strategy documents to sharpen his thinking.

**8. open monitoring** — theo dõi mở / quan sát rộng
*Dùng khi: mô tả phong cách thiền không chọn lọc đối tượng*
> Open monitoring practice involves observing thoughts and sensations without fixing on any single one.
> Advanced meditators often shift from focused attention to open monitoring as their practice deepens.
> Open monitoring cultivates the receptive awareness useful for creative problem-solving.

**9. sitting practice** — buổi thiền ngồi
*Dùng khi: chỉ một phiên thiền định thường ngày*
> Her sitting practice lasts twenty minutes every morning before the children wake up.
> Missing a single sitting practice is fine; missing a week is where habits begin to erode.
> He journals immediately after his sitting practice while insights are still fresh.

**10. formal practice** — thực hành chính thức / thiền có chủ ý
*Dùng khi: phân biệt thiền đúng nghĩa với mindfulness trong sinh hoạt*
> Formal practice means setting aside dedicated time to meditate, as opposed to informal mindfulness.
> Even fifteen minutes of formal practice a day produces measurable changes in cognitive flexibility.
> She views her morning run as supplementary but keeps formal practice non-negotiable.

**11. breath as anchor** — hơi thở là điểm neo
*Dùng khi: giải thích tại sao hơi thở được dùng làm tâm điểm*
> Using the breath as anchor means you always have a home base to return to when the mind wanders.
> The simplicity of the breath as anchor makes it accessible regardless of experience level.
> When anxiety rises before a keynote, treating the breath as anchor can ground you in seconds.

**12. return your focus** — đưa sự chú ý trở lại
*Dùng khi: nói về hành động nhận ra sự xao nhãng và quay về*
> Each time you notice your mind has wandered, simply return your focus to the breath without judgment.
> The ability to return your focus quickly is the core skill that meditation develops.
> She trained herself to return her focus within seconds of noticing she was distracted in meetings.

**13. bell sound** — tiếng chuông (thiền)
*Dùng khi: nói về tín hiệu bắt đầu hoặc kết thúc buổi thiền*
> Many meditation apps use a soft bell sound to mark the beginning and end of a session.
> The bell sound invites participants to pause and arrive in the present moment.
> He installed a bell sound on his computer that rings every hour as a mindfulness reminder.

**14. meditation cushion** — nệm thiền / bồ đoàn
*Dùng khi: đề cập đến vật dụng hỗ trợ tư thế ngồi thiền*
> A good meditation cushion keeps the hips elevated so the lower back stays naturally upright.
> She keeps her meditation cushion on the office couch as a visible cue to practice.
> You don't need an expensive meditation cushion; a folded blanket works just as well.

**15. mindful breathing** — thở có ý thức / hít thở chánh niệm
*Dùng khi: nói về hành động thở có chủ ý và chú tâm*
> Mindful breathing is the fastest way to activate the parasympathetic nervous system.
> She introduced mindful breathing as a two-minute ritual before every team retrospective.
> Mindful breathing doesn't require closing your eyes — you can do it mid-conversation.

**16. meditation timer** — đồng hồ hẹn giờ thiền
*Dùng khi: nói về công cụ hỗ trợ quản lý thời gian thiền*
> A meditation timer removes the temptation to peek at the clock and breaks the illusion that time is dragging.
> He sets a meditation timer for twenty minutes and commits to staying seated no matter what arises.
> Most meditation apps include a customizable meditation timer with interval bells.

**17. spine erect** — cột sống thẳng
*Dùng khi: mô tả tư thế đúng trong thiền ngồi*
> Keeping the spine erect without being rigid allows alert awareness without tension.
> The teacher reminded the group to sit with the spine erect, as if a string were pulling the crown upward.
> A spine erect posture signals wakefulness to the nervous system, reducing the risk of drowsiness.

**18. settle the mind** — lắng dịu tâm trí
*Dùng khi: mô tả quá trình tâm trí dần yên tĩnh khi bắt đầu thiền*
> Give yourself the first two minutes just to settle the mind before trying to focus.
> It can take a week of daily practice before you reliably settle the mind within the session.
> She uses a short breathing exercise to settle the mind before difficult one-on-one conversations.

**19. background noise** — tiếng ồn nền / tiếng ồn xung quanh
*Dùng khi: thảo luận về thách thức thiền trong môi trường không yên tĩnh*
> Treating background noise as just another sensation rather than an obstacle is a mark of mature practice.
> Some practitioners deliberately meditate with background noise to build equanimity.
> The office background noise that once frustrated him became a neutral backdrop after six months of practice.

**20. begin again** — bắt đầu lại (không phán xét)
*Dùng khi: khuyến khích thái độ không chỉ trích khi tâm trí xao nhãng*
> The entire practice can be summarized in two words: begin again, every time the mind wanders.
> Begin again is not a consolation phrase; it is literally the mechanism through which the mind is trained.
> When his meditation habit lapsed for a month, he simply chose to begin again the next morning.

**21. mantra repetition** — lặp mantra / câu thần chú
*Dùng khi: giới thiệu kỹ thuật thiền dùng âm thanh hoặc câu chữ*
> Transcendental Meditation is built around silent mantra repetition as the primary technique.
> She uses mantra repetition in the shower to set a calm, intentional tone before work.
> Mantra repetition gives the thinking mind a job, which paradoxically quiets it.

**22. eyes half-open** — mắt hé mở
*Dùng khi: mô tả tư thế mắt trong một số truyền thống thiền định*
> Zen tradition often keeps the eyes half-open and cast downward to maintain alertness.
> Meditating with eyes half-open can be helpful for people who tend to fall asleep with eyes closed.
> He experimented with eyes half-open and found it reduced daydreaming during sessions.

**23. daily sit** — buổi ngồi thiền hằng ngày
*Dùng khi: nói về thói quen thiền định mỗi ngày một cách thân mật*
> His daily sit is the one commitment that has survived every organizational restructure in his career.
> Even a five-minute daily sit is more valuable than an hour-long session once a week.
> She protects her morning daily sit the same way she protects a critical product review.

**24. inner stillness** — sự tĩnh lặng bên trong
*Dùng khi: mô tả trạng thái yên bình nội tâm đạt được qua thiền*
> Inner stillness is not the absence of thought but the ability to remain undisturbed by thought.
> Leaders who cultivate inner stillness tend to make higher-quality decisions under pressure.
> She described the retreat as the first time she had tasted genuine inner stillness in years.

**25. present-moment awareness** — nhận thức khoảnh khắc hiện tại
*Dùng khi: mô tả mục tiêu cốt lõi của chánh niệm*
> Present-moment awareness is the antidote to the chronic distraction that plagues modern knowledge work.
> He trains present-moment awareness through brief pauses throughout the workday.
> Present-moment awareness doesn't mean ignoring the future — it means planning the future with a clear mind.

## Letting Go & Acceptance

**26. let it go** — buông bỏ / để nó trôi qua
*Dùng khi: khuyến khích không bám víu vào suy nghĩ hoặc cảm xúc*
> In meditation, "let it go" means neither suppressing a thought nor following it — just noticing and releasing.
> She learned to let it go when a project failed, which made her a more resilient leader.
> The hardest part of the practice is learning to let it go without forcing it.

**27. non-attachment** — không bám víu / vô chấp
*Dùng khi: giải thích thái độ buông xả đối với kết quả hoặc trải nghiệm*
> Non-attachment doesn't mean indifference; it means caring deeply about outcomes without clinging to them.
> His colleagues noticed how non-attachment to his own ideas made him a better listener in design reviews.
> Practicing non-attachment to quarterly metrics reduces the anxiety that clouds strategic thinking.

**28. radical acceptance** — chấp nhận triệt để
*Dùng khi: nói về việc chấp nhận hoàn toàn thực tế như nó đang là*
> Radical acceptance of a failed launch freed the team to pivot quickly rather than stay in denial.
> She describes radical acceptance not as giving up but as the prerequisite for effective action.
> Radical acceptance means acknowledging what is true, not approving of it.

**29. release resistance** — buông bỏ sự kháng cự
*Dùng khi: mô tả quá trình ngừng chống lại cảm xúc hoặc tình huống khó chịu*
> The moment he chose to release resistance to the board's feedback, a creative solution appeared.
> Release resistance doesn't mean compliance — it means not wasting energy on what cannot be changed.
> She coaches executives to release resistance before entering difficult negotiations.

**30. sit with discomfort** — ngồi cùng sự khó chịu
*Dùng khi: nói về việc chịu đựng cảm giác khó chịu mà không phản ứng tức thì*
> The ability to sit with discomfort during a meditation session translates directly to boardroom composure.
> He practiced sitting with discomfort for ten minutes a day until uncertainty stopped feeling catastrophic.
> Sitting with discomfort is a skill, not a personality trait — it can be trained.

**31. impermanence** — vô thường
*Dùng khi: nhắc đến nguyên lý mọi thứ đều thay đổi và không bền vững*
> Understanding impermanence helps leaders avoid over-investing emotionally in any single outcome.
> The insight of impermanence makes success sweeter and failure more bearable.
> She returned from the retreat with a vivid sense of impermanence that sharpened her priorities.

**32. clinging to outcomes** — bám víu vào kết quả
*Dùng khi: nói về xu hướng gắn chặt bản sắc với kết quả công việc*
> Clinging to outcomes is the chief source of anxiety for high-performing professionals.
> The practice teaches you to act with full effort while clinging to outcomes less and less.
> He noticed that clinging to outcomes made him risk-averse in a market that rewarded bold bets.

**33. allow things to be** — để mọi thứ như nó là
*Dùng khi: mô tả thái độ không can thiệp, không phán xét trong thiền*
> The simplest meditation instruction is this: allow things to be exactly as they are.
> She learned to allow things to be during the uncertainty of a company acquisition.
> Allowing things to be is not passive resignation; it is the starting point for clear-eyed action.

**34. with equanimity** — với tâm bình thản
*Dùng khi: nói về khả năng đối diện khó khăn mà không bị kéo đi cảm xúc*
> He handled the investor's criticism with equanimity that surprised the entire room.
> Equanimity is not coldness — it is the emotional steadiness that makes good leadership possible.
> She meets both praise and setback with equanimity, which her team finds deeply stabilizing.

**35. without judgment** — không phán xét
*Dùng khi: mô tả thái độ quan sát trung lập trong chánh niệm*
> Observing thoughts without judgment is the central attitude of mindfulness practice.
> She listens to team concerns without judgment, which creates psychological safety.
> He notes his emotional reactions without judgment and then chooses how to respond.

**36. open hands** — bàn tay mở / tâm thái buông mở
*Dùng khi: dùng hình ảnh ẩn dụ về việc không nắm giữ, không ép buộc*
> The metaphor of open hands captures the non-grasping quality that mindfulness cultivates.
> She approaches her career with open hands, curious about what each chapter will bring.
> Holding a strategy with open hands means being committed to the goal but flexible about the path.

**37. soften around it** — dịu lại xung quanh điều đó / mềm mỏng hơn
*Dùng khi: hướng dẫn giảm sức căng đối với cảm giác khó chịu*
> Instead of pushing the sensation away, the teacher said: soften around it and let it move.
> He learned to soften around frustration rather than suppress or amplify it.
> When anxiety arises, soften around it — that shift alone reduces its intensity by half.

**38. observe without reacting** — quan sát mà không phản ứng
*Dùng khi: nói về việc tạo khoảng cách giữa kích thích và hành động*
> Learning to observe without reacting is perhaps the most career-relevant skill meditation builds.
> She can now observe without reacting when a team member challenges her publicly.
> Observe without reacting is the antidote to the trigger-response loops that derail leaders.

**39. embrace uncertainty** — đón nhận sự bất định
*Dùng khi: nói về thái độ sẵn sàng với điều chưa biết trong công việc hoặc cuộc sống*
> The ability to embrace uncertainty is a competitive advantage in fast-moving markets.
> Mindfulness trained him to embrace uncertainty rather than paper over it with false confidence.
> She coaches her product team to embrace uncertainty as the creative ground from which innovation grows.

**40. soften the grip** — nới lỏng sự nắm giữ
*Dùng khi: ẩn dụ về việc giảm bớt sự kiểm soát quá mức*
> Great leadership often requires softening the grip on how you expected things to unfold.
> She had to soften the grip on her original roadmap when user research revealed a different need.
> Softening the grip on approval-seeking was the biggest shift in his executive presence.

**41. flow with resistance** — thuận theo sức cản
*Dùng khi: nói về chiến lược tiếp nhận thay vì đối đầu với khó khăn*
> Rather than fighting the board's pushback, she chose to flow with resistance and find common ground.
> Flow with resistance is not capitulation; it is the art of using opposing forces creatively.
> Practicing flow with resistance in meditation made him far more agile in stakeholder negotiations.

**42. meet it with kindness** — đón nhận nó bằng lòng tử tế
*Dùng khi: hướng dẫn thái độ từ bi với bản thân khi gặp khó khăn*
> When self-criticism arises in practice, the instruction is simply to meet it with kindness.
> She learned to meet fatigue with kindness rather than judging herself for not doing more.
> Meet it with kindness is also good leadership advice — meet a struggling team member's gap with kindness.

**43. release control** — buông bỏ sự kiểm soát
*Dùng khi: nói về việc học cách không cần kiểm soát mọi kết quả*
> The paradox of leadership is that releasing control often generates more team ownership and output.
> He had to release control of the project timeline when a dependency changed unexpectedly.
> Meditation teaches you to release control of the mind's content while remaining attentive.

**44. the gap between stimulus and response** — khoảng cách giữa kích thích và phản ứng
*Dùng khi: giải thích cơ chế mindfulness giúp ứng phó thông minh hơn*
> Viktor Frankl's insight about the gap between stimulus and response is the foundation of mindful leadership.
> Mindfulness practice literally widens the gap between stimulus and response in the brain.
> She says her practice gave her a half-second gap between stimulus and response that changed everything.

**45. this too shall pass** — cái này rồi cũng sẽ qua
*Dùng khi: nhắc đến tính vô thường như một nguồn an ủi trong khó khăn*
> Whenever anxiety peaked before a product launch, he reminded himself: this too shall pass.
> This too shall pass is not denial; it is the honest recognition of impermanence.
> She wrote "this too shall pass" on her whiteboard during the company's most difficult quarter.

## States of Mind

**46. monkey mind** — tâm viên / tâm trí xáo động
*Dùng khi: mô tả trạng thái tâm trí liên tục nhảy từ ý nghĩ này sang ý nghĩ khác*
> Everyone experiences monkey mind; the practice is noticing it sooner and returning to focus.
> Her monkey mind was especially active at 3 a.m., rehearsing every risk in the product launch.
> Meditation doesn't eliminate monkey mind; it just makes you less carried away by it.

**47. mental chatter** — tiếng ồn trong đầu / lẩm bẩm nội tâm
*Dùng khi: nói về dòng suy nghĩ không ngừng trong tâm trí*
> The goal is not to silence mental chatter but to relate to it differently — as background noise rather than command.
> She noticed that mental chatter peaked in the fifteen minutes before a board call.
> Reducing mental chatter through practice creates space for more original thinking.

**48. heightened awareness** — nhận thức nâng cao / tỉnh táo hơn bình thường
*Dùng khi: mô tả trạng thái chú ý sắc bén hơn*
> Long retreat practitioners report a heightened awareness of color, sound, and sensation.
> Heightened awareness during a product review lets her catch nuances in user feedback others miss.
> Even a brief meditation produces a measurable period of heightened awareness afterward.

**49. calm abiding** — an trú / định
*Dùng khi: nói về trạng thái tâm ổn định và tập trung sâu*
> Calm abiding is the classical meditation state in which the mind rests naturally on its object.
> He described the second week of retreat as the first time he experienced genuine calm abiding.
> Calm abiding is not forced stillness; it arises when effort is relaxed at the right moment.

**50. flow state** — trạng thái dòng chảy / trạng thái flow
*Dùng khi: nói về trạng thái làm việc hoàn toàn đắm mình, không bị xao nhãng*
> Mindfulness practice makes flow state more accessible by training the attention muscle.
> He enters flow state most reliably after a twenty-minute morning meditation.
> The product team's best sprints happen when the team collectively enters a kind of shared flow state.

**51. wandering mind** — tâm trí lang thang
*Dùng khi: mô tả xu hướng tự nhiên của tâm trí đi chệch khỏi hiện tại*
> Research shows the wandering mind is active nearly half of our waking hours.
> A wandering mind during a strategy session is costly — mindfulness training reduces it.
> She tracks how quickly she catches her wandering mind as a proxy for her practice quality.

**52. alert and relaxed** — tỉnh táo và thư giãn
*Dùng khi: mô tả trạng thái lý tưởng khi thiền hoặc làm việc sáng tạo*
> The meditation ideal is to be simultaneously alert and relaxed — neither drowsy nor tense.
> She describes her best decision-making as happening when she is alert and relaxed, not under pressure.
> Alert and relaxed is the neurological state where insight most readily arises.

**53. overstimulation** — kích thích quá mức
*Dùng khi: nói về tình trạng não bộ tiếp nhận quá nhiều đầu vào*
> Chronic overstimulation from notifications, meetings, and media is the main obstacle to clear thinking.
> He built a mindfulness practice specifically to counter overstimulation from back-to-back calls.
> Overstimulation narrows perspective; stepping back to breathe literally broadens it.

**54. cognitive overload** — quá tải nhận thức
*Dùng khi: mô tả tình trạng xử lý thông tin vượt khả năng*
> Cognitive overload is endemic in tech leadership — mindfulness is one of the few proven antidotes.
> She recognized cognitive overload as the culprit when her decision quality dropped in Q4.
> Brief mindfulness breaks interrupt cognitive overload before it compounds into burnout.

**55. emotional reactivity** — tính phản ứng cảm xúc cao
*Dùng khi: nói về xu hướng bị kéo đi bởi cảm xúc mạnh*
> Meditation measurably reduces emotional reactivity by thickening the prefrontal cortex.
> His team noticed a decline in his emotional reactivity after six months of consistent practice.
> High emotional reactivity is often misread as passion, but it undermines strategic credibility.

**56. stress response** — phản ứng căng thẳng / phản ứng stress
*Dùng khi: nói về phản ứng sinh lý của cơ thể trước áp lực*
> The stress response evolved for physical threats, but modern minds trigger it with emails.
> Mindful breathing is one of the fastest ways to deactivate the stress response.
> She monitors her stress response as data — not something to suppress but to understand.

**57. parasympathetic state** — trạng thái thần kinh đối giao cảm / trạng thái nghỉ ngơi
*Dùng khi: giải thích cơ chế sinh lý của thư giãn sâu*
> Deep breathing activates the parasympathetic state, which improves creativity and judgment.
> The goal of his wind-down routine is to shift into a parasympathetic state before sleep.
> Mindfulness practices reliably induce the parasympathetic state, reducing cortisol levels.

**58. beginner's mind** — tâm thái người mới bắt đầu
*Dùng khi: nói về thái độ cởi mở, tò mò như lần đầu trải nghiệm*
> Shunryu Suzuki's concept of beginner's mind is especially valuable in fast-changing tech markets.
> She approaches each product problem with beginner's mind, deliberately suspending prior assumptions.
> Beginner's mind is not naivety; it is the discipline of remaining genuinely curious.

**59. clear seeing** — nhìn thấu / thấy rõ ràng
*Dùng khi: mô tả khả năng nhận thức không bị bóp méo bởi cảm xúc hoặc thiên kiến*
> Meditation practice cultivates clear seeing by reducing the distortion caused by habitual reactivity.
> She credits her clear seeing of team dynamics to years of contemplative practice.
> Clear seeing is what separates a response from a reaction.

**60. mental fog** — sương mù tâm trí / đầu óc mờ nhạt
*Dùng khi: mô tả trạng thái tư duy không minh mẫn, khó tập trung*
> Mental fog after a red-eye flight is a genuine impairment — mindfulness can reduce but not eliminate it.
> She treats mental fog as a signal to pause rather than push harder.
> A ten-minute meditation often lifts mental fog more effectively than a third cup of coffee.

**61. inner critic** — tiếng nói phê phán bên trong
*Dùng khi: nói về giọng nói tự chỉ trích trong tâm trí*
> Mindfulness helps you observe the inner critic as a habitual voice rather than objective truth.
> His inner critic was loudest during performance review season; practice gave him distance from it.
> She learned to treat her inner critic as useful feedback filtered through her own biases.

**62. default mode network** — mạng lưới não bộ mặc định
*Dùng khi: giải thích khoa học thần kinh đằng sau tâm trí lang thang*
> The default mode network is the brain's "background chatter" system that meditation quiets.
> Activity in the default mode network is linked to rumination and reduced present-moment focus.
> Meditation reduces default mode network activity, which correlates with improved wellbeing.

**63. alert presence** — sự hiện diện tỉnh thức
*Dùng khi: mô tả chất lượng chú ý hoàn toàn vào đây và bây giờ*
> Her alert presence in meetings communicates respect and generates sharper insights.

**124. layers of self** — các tầng bản thân / nhiều chiều của bản ngã

## Mindful Leadership

**126. psychological safety** — an toàn tâm lý

**127. compassionate leadership** — lãnh đạo bằng lòng từ bi

> Compassionate leadership is not soft; research shows it drives higher team engagement and retention.
> She shifted from directive leadership to compassionate leadership after her mindfulness training.
> Compassionate leadership means caring about the person, not just the deliverable.

**128. hold space** — giữ không gian / tạo không gian an toàn
*Dùng khi: nói về hành động tạo điều kiện để người khác bày tỏ mà không phán xét*
> The best one-on-ones she leads begin with her deliberately holding space before moving to agenda.
> Holding space means staying present with another person's experience without trying to fix it.
> He learned to hold space for his team's frustration rather than immediately problem-solving.

**129. lead with presence** — lãnh đạo bằng sự hiện diện

> She leads with presence — people feel it the moment she enters a room.

**130. conscious leadership** — lãnh đạo có ý thức
*Dùng khi: nói về phong cách lãnh đạo có chủ ý, tự giác cao*
> Conscious leadership requires the self-awareness to know how you are showing up in every interaction.
> She describes conscious leadership as the practice of leading from values rather than from fear.
> Conscious leadership is not a personality type — it is a set of practices available to anyone.

**131. servant leadership** — lãnh đạo phục vụ
*Dùng khi: nói về phong cách lãnh đạo đặt nhu cầu của đội ngũ lên trên*
> Robert Greenleaf's servant leadership model resonates with mindfulness practitioners because both center others.
> She shifted to servant leadership after realizing her team's growth was the product she was building.
> Servant leadership asks: "How can I remove obstacles and support this person's best work?"

**132. emotional intelligence** — trí tuệ cảm xúc
*Dùng khi: nói về tập hợp kỹ năng quản lý cảm xúc của bản thân và người khác*
> Daniel Goleman's framework for emotional intelligence aligns closely with mindfulness competencies.
> She views her meditation practice as the daily training for her emotional intelligence.
> Emotional intelligence is more predictive of leadership effectiveness than IQ across most roles.

**133. the pause before you act** — dừng lại trước khi hành động

> The pause before you act is where leadership happens — in that gap between impulse and decision.
> She institutionalized the pause before you act by ending every heated meeting with a night's sleep rule.
> He trains the pause before you act in meditation by simply noticing the urge to scratch before moving.

**134. team wellbeing** — phúc lợi của đội ngũ
*Dùng khi: nói về sức khỏe tổng thể — thể chất và tinh thần — của nhóm làm việc*
